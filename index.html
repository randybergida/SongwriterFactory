Randy Bergida is a Brooklyn-based musician and songwriter who began recording his first songs in 1999 while living in a log cabin in the Colestien Valley of Mount Ashland. Rent was little, and hitchhiking into town and playing in cafés sustained his way. He moved to Portland, Oregon in 2004, where his former band, Skidmore Fountain, laid roots and recorded their first record with Tape Op legend Larry Crane (Elliot Smith, Stephen Malkmus). That same year, while touring across the states, he made it to New York and decided to stay, settling in his home of Greenpoint, Brooklyn.

Randy has been professionally performing, teaching, writing, recording, and producing music for over 20 years. His music has been featured in AM New York and CMJ, played on hundreds of radio stations across the country, charted on FMBQ, and was the winner of Airplay Direct’s “All Things Digital.” He has been featured on MTV, named an “iTunes favorite album,” and recognized as a CD Baby staff favorite.

Beyond his early work, Randy has recorded with industry heavyweights, including multi-Grammy award-winning producer Ken Lewis (John Legend, David Byrne, Beastie Boys) and Joel Hamilton (Tom Waits, Dub Trio, Blackroc/The Black Keys). He released Firebug under his own name in 2009, founded The Letter Yellow in 2012—releasing Walking Down the Streets (2012), the 100% analog Watercolor Overcast (2015), and On the Other Side (2018)—and released the debut record for New Indiana, Darkness Sunshine, in 2018 with longtime collaborator Topu Lyo. He continues to write and release records prolifically with Brooklyn String Duo.

Randy regularly performs in New York’s most coveted music venues. Today, his focus is on mentoring the next generation of songwriters through songwriting, guitar, and voice lessons in Brooklyn (Williamsburg/Greenpoint). In addition to teaching, Randy offers specialized production and recording services at Songwriter Factory. The studio is equipped with handpicked, vintage-flavored, top-of-the-line analog and digital gear, designed to provide a rich musical atmosphere for refining your skills as a songwriter and vocalist. Randy holds a degree in composition and guitar from the University of Arizona.
