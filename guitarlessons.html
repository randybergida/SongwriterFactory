Personalized Guitar Lessons with Randy Bergida in Greenpoint, Brooklyn

I remember walking around everywhere with my guitar. I would play at every moment of free time I had. I thought if I always had my guitar strapped to me, those extra minutes would translate into a better guitarist.

But what I think that really did is form the foundation of my philosophy: that the guitar is an extension of myself. It's the feeling of knowing your instrument not just as something you play, but something you feel. Something that feels as familiar as your daily cup of tea, your morning espresso, or a warm bowl of oatmeal.

The Philosophy Behind My Teaching

While those days of never letting my guitar leave my side created that initial comfort, I later learned that true growth comes down to the quality of practice. The intention, the goal, and the map to achieve it.

All of my students have their individual goals. My job as a music instructor is to simplify that journey so that it’s fun, and the ultimate outcome is making great and honest music.

I love that the simplest thing can change a guitar lesson into a master class. The happiest moments are when you blow a student's mind by connecting a dot, and they leave forever a changed musician.

Who I Work With

I love working with students whose goals—no matter how far from it they feel—are to make songs and record records. That can happen even with a complete beginner. You never know until you try.

Sometimes playing guitar, writing songs, and singing comes easy, and sometimes it takes immense work. Either way, the goal is to feel joy when playing guitar and making music, and that is at the heart of how I teach in my studio.
